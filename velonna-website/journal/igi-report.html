The Journal · Lab-grown, explained

Your certificate, line by line.

Every Velonna stone comes with an IGI grading report. Here is how to read it — and how to verify it is real.

What is IGI?

Founded in 1975, the International Gemological Institute is the world's leading grading laboratory for lab-grown diamonds, with reports recognised by retailers and insurers globally. IGI grading is entirely anonymous — all owner information is masked during the process. Multiple gemologists examine each stone independently, and a grade is only assigned when there are sufficient agreeing opinions. The result is a report you can trust.

Reading the report

Report number The stone's unique ID. Use this to verify the certificate at igi.org. Always confirm it matches the laser inscription on the stone itself.
Shape & cutting style The physical form of the stone — e.g. Round Brilliant, Oval Modified Brilliant, Pear Shaped.
Measurements Length × Width × Depth in millimetres. Tells you the actual physical size of the stone.
Carat weight The precise weight of the stone. 1 carat = 0.2 grams.
Colour grade Graded D to Z. Multiple IGI gemologists submit opinions independently. Grade is confirmed when there are sufficient agreeing assessments.
Clarity grade Graded FL to I3, assessed at 10× magnification. The report includes a plotted diagram — inclusions marked in red, surface blemishes in green.
Cut grade Ranges from Excellent to Poor. The most important grade for real-world beauty.
Polish Smoothness of the stone's facet surfaces. Excellent to Poor.
Symmetry Precision of facet alignment. Excellent to Poor.
Fluorescence How the stone reacts under UV light. None is the most common and most neutral.
Laser inscription The report number laser-engraved on the girdle of the stone — the permanent link between the diamond and its certificate.
Comments Any additional grader notes, such as "hearts and arrows" or treatment disclosures.

Verifying your certificate

Every IGI report can be verified in seconds at igi.org using the report number. Scan the QR code on the certificate or enter the number manually. A genuine report will display the full grading details online. Always verify before purchasing. If a seller cannot provide a verifiable IGI, GIA, or AGS certificate — walk away.

Lab-grown notation

IGI reports for lab-grown diamonds are identical in format and grading rigour to those for natural diamonds. The only difference: the report clearly states "Laboratory Grown." Same standards. Same scrutiny. Full transparency.
