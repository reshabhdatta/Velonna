The Journal · Lab-grown, explained

Same carbon. Two very different stories.

Every Velonna stone is lab-grown. Here is exactly what that means — and what it doesn't.

What makes them identical

Lab-grown diamonds have the same chemical composition, crystal structure, and physical properties as natural diamonds — pure carbon arranged in a crystal lattice. This is what gives both their exceptional hardness, brilliance, and fire. Both are optically identical and will look the same to the naked eye. Only specialised testing equipment can tell them apart.

How they are made

Lab-grown diamonds begin as tiny carbon seeds from pre-existing diamonds. Two primary methods are used: High Pressure High Temperature (HPHT) and Chemical Vapour Deposition (CVD). Both recreate the conditions that allow carbon atoms to form into diamond crystal. After several days or weeks, a rough diamond is created — then cut, polished, graded, and set in jewellery exactly like a natural diamond.

Price

Lab-grown diamonds now cost up to 73% less than natural diamonds of the same carat, cut, and clarity. In 2025, a round 1-carat lab-grown diamond costs approximately $845, compared to around $3,895 for a comparable natural stone. That means a significantly larger or higher-quality stone for the same budget.

Resale value

Lab-grown diamonds are produced in abundance, meaning their resale value is less predictable over time. Neither lab-grown nor mined diamonds hold value the way gold does. If long-term resale is your primary goal, gold is the safer investment. If beauty, quality, and value for money matter most — lab-grown wins.

Are they real diamonds?

Yes. Lab-grown diamonds possess the same chemical properties and hardness as naturally mined diamonds, and are subject to the same 4Cs grading system. Every Velonna stone is certified to the same international standards as a mined diamond.

The numbers

In 2024, 52% of couples chose a lab-grown diamond for their engagement ring — up 40% since 2019. Lab-grown is no longer the alternative. It is the choice of the modern buyer.
